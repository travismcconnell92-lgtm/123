Time-Sensitive: Florida construction liens must be recorded within 90 days of your last date of furnishing labor, materials, or services. Missing this deadline permanently extinguishes your lien rights. Do not delay.

Why It Matters

Why Contractors Use Attorney-Prepared Liens

✔

Avoid Costly Errors

Incorrect legal descriptions, owner information, or statutory language can invalidate your lien entirely.

📋

Comply With Florida Law

All construction liens are drafted to satisfy strict Florida statutory requirements under Chapter 713.

🔒

Protect Your Payment Rights

A properly prepared lien ensures your ability to enforce payment if a dispute reaches the courts.

💲

Flat Fee Preparation

Predictable pricing makes construction lien preparation simple and accessible for contractors of all sizes.

Common Scenarios

Common Situations Where Contractors File Liens

💰

Unpaid Invoice by Owner

The property owner refuses to pay the final invoice despite completed work.

⚖️

Dispute Over Payment

The owner or general contractor contests the amount owed for work performed.

📄

Change Order Conflicts

Disagreements arise over additional work or modifications to the original contract.

⏳

Delayed or Withheld Payment

The property owner repeatedly delays payment even after multiple requests.
